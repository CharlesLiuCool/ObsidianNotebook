>**Definition** Let ${S \subseteq \Bbb{R}^n}$ and ${\underline{x} \in \Bbb{R}^n}$. (${\underline{x}}$ not necessarily in ${S}$). ${\underline{x}}$ is an *adherent point* of ${S}$ if ${B(\underline{x}, r) \cap S \neq \emptyset \;\forall\;r>0}$.

**Example**
1. If ${\underline{x} \in S}$, then ${\underline{x}}$ is an adherent point of ${S}$.
2. Suppose ${\text{sup}\,S = a < \infty}$, for ${S \subseteq \Bbb{R}}$. Then ${a}$ is an adherent point of ${S}$.

>**Definition** Let ${S \subseteq \Bbb{R}^n}$ and ${\underline{x} \in \Bbb{R}^n}$.
${\underline{x}}$ is an *accumulation point* of ${S}$ if ${B(\underline{x},r) \cap (S \setminus \{\underline{x}\}) \neq \emptyset, \forall\;r > 0}$.

i.e. ${\underline{x}}$ is an accumulation point of ${S}$ if and only if ${\underline{x}}$ adheres to ${S \setminus \{\underline{x}\}}$.

If ${\underline{x} \in S}$ but ${\underline{x}}$ is *not* an accumulation point of ${S}$, then ${\underline{x}}$ is called an isolated point of ${S}$.

**Example** ${S = \{\frac{1}{n} \,|\, n = 1,2,3,...\}}$
${0 \notin S}$, ${0}$ is an adherent and accumulation point of ${S}$.

**Example** Every ${r \in \Bbb{R}}$ is an accumulation point of ${\Bbb{Q}}$.

**Example** ${S = \{0\} \cup (1,2]}$
adherent points of ${S:\{0\} \cup [1,2]}$
accumulation points of ${S: [1,2]}$

>**Theorem.** Let ${\underline{x}}$ be an accumulation point of ${S}$. Then every ${B(\underline{x}, r)}$ contains infinitely many points of ${S}$.

(Hint) ${\exists \underline{a}_1 \in B(\underline{x},r) \cap (S \setminus \{\underline{x}\})}$

Let ${r_1 = || \underline{x} - \underline{a}_1||}$

${\exists \underline{a_2} \in B(x,\frac{r_1}{2}) \cap (S \setminus \{\underline{x}\})}$

*Proof:* Assume the contrary.
i.e. ${\exists r > 0}$ such that
${B(\underline{x},r) \cap (S \setminus \{\underline{x}\}) = \{\underline{a_1}, \underline{a_2}, ..., \underline{a_n}\}}$ (there is finitely many points).

Let ${r_0 = \min\{||\underline{x} - \underline{a_1}||,..., ||\underline{x} - \underline{a_n}||\}}$

Then ${B(\underline{x}, \frac{r_0}{2}) \cap (S \setminus \{\underline{x}\}) = \emptyset}$
${\rightarrow\!\leftarrow \underline{x}}$ is an accumulation point of ${S}$.
