**Theorem.** Chernoff Bound
Let $X = \sum\limits_{i=1}^n X_i$ be a random variable, where $X_i = 1$ with probability $p$ and $0$ with probability $1-p$ and all $X_i$ are independent. Let $\mu = \Bbb{E}[x] = \sum\limits_{i=1}^n p_i$. Then

*Upper Tail*
$$\Pr[X \ge (1-\delta)\mu] \le e^{\frac{\delta^2}{\delta + 2}\mu}$$
for all $\delta > 0$

*Lower Tail*
$$\Pr[X \le (1-\delta)\mu] \le e^{\mu\delta^2/2}$$
for all $0 < \delta < 1$
