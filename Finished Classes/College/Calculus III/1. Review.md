#### Dot Product

The **dot product** of ${\vec{u} = \langle u_1,\;u_2,\;u_3\rangle \text{ and } \vec{v} = \langle v_1,\;v_2,\;v_3\rangle \text{ is}}$

$${\vec{u} \cdot \vec{v} = u_1v_1 + u_2v_2 + u_3v_3 \;\;\text{ a number, or a scalar}}$$

### Recall:

${\vec{u} \cdot \vec{v} = |\vec{u}|\,|\vec{v}|\,\cos\theta}$

## Theorem 13.2

Properties of the **Dot Product**

Suppose ${{\bf u}, {\bf v}, {\bf w}}$ are vectors and let ${c}$ be a scalar.
1. ${{\bf u} \cdot {\bf v} = {\bf v} \cdot {\bf u}}$ Commutative Property
2. ${c({\bf u} \cdot {\bf v}) = (c{\bf u}) \cdot {\bf v} = {\bf u} \cdot (c{\bf v})}$ Associative Property
3. ${{\bf u} \cdot ({\bf v} + {\bf w}) = {\bf u} \cdot {\bf v} + {\bf u} \cdot {\bf w}}$ Distributive Property
