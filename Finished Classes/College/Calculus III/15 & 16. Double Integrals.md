>[!question] 23
>Evaluate 
>$${\int_0^1\int_1^4\dfrac{3y}{\sqrt{x+y^2}}dx\,dy}$$

>[!check]- Solution
>**Inner:**
>$${\int_1^4 3y \cdot (x+y^2)^{-1/2} dx}$$
>$${3y \cdot 2(x+y^2)^{1/2} \bigg|_{x=1}^{x=4} = 6y(4+y^2)^{1/2} - 6y(1+y^2)^{1/2}}$$
>**Outer:**
>$${\begin{align} &\int_0^1 (6y(4+y^2)^{1/2} - 6y(12y^2)^{1/2})dy \\ &= \int_0^1 6y(4+y^2)^{1/2} dy - \int_0^1 6y(12y^2)^{1/2}dy \\ &= \int_0^1 6y(4+y^2)^{1/2} dy - \int_0^1 6y(1+y^2)^{1/2}dy \\ &= \int_4^5 3u^{1/2}du - \int_1^2 3u^{1/2}du \\ &= 2u^{3/2} \bigg|_4^5 - 2u^{3/2} \bigg|_1^2 = 2 \cdot 5^{3/2} - 16 - 2 \cdot 2^{3/2} + 2 \cdot 1 \\ &= 10\sqrt{5} - 16 - 4\sqrt{2} - 2 \end{align}}$$


## Theorem 16.1: (Fubini) Double Integrals over Rectangular Regions
Let ${f}$ be continuous on the rectangular region ${R = \{(x,y): a \leq x \leq b, c \leq y \leq d\}}$. The double integral of ${f}$ over ${R}$ may be evaluated either of two iterated integrals:
$${\int \int f(x,y) dA = \int_c^d \int_a^b f(x,y) dx\,dy = \int_a^b \int _c^d f(x,y) dy\,dx}$$

>[!question] 47
>Compute the average value of ${f(x,y) = e^{-y}}$ over the region ${R = \{(x,y): 0 \leq x \leq 6, 0 \leq y \leq \ln(2)\}}$.

>[!check]- Solution
>Recall that 
>(avg. value of ${f(x,y)}$ on ${R}$)${= \bar{f} = \dfrac{\int \int f(x,y) dA}{\text{ area of R }}}$
>area of K = ${6 \ln 2}$
>So 
>$${\bar{f} = \dfrac{1}{6\ln(2)} \int_0^6 \int _0^{\ln(2)} e^{-y} dydx = \dfrac{1}{2\ln(2)}}$$

## Theorem 16.2: Double Integrals over General (non-rectangular) Regions
**Idea:**
$${\int_0^8 \int_0^2 f(x,y)dy\,dx = \iint\limits_R f(x,y) dA}$$
